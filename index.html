My Investing Background


Hello, World! I have always been rather ambitious with my aspirations of becoming a full-time independent investor. Perhaps a little too ambitious, as I became fully invested in financial markets towards the end of 2017. From there I let my obsession control me, dictating much of my free time. Although unhealthy, it was certainly beneifical in terms of intellectual growth. I have been investing for almost a year now, and the experience is invaluable. I would like to think that I am ahead of the curve for finance undergraduates, but I know there is still a long, uphill climb that awaits me.


My Investing Plan


In the forseeable future, I plan on majoring in finance, and getting a minor in real estate, all within three years time, which is a year less than the average time a degree requires. With my extra year, I am going to return home and begin growing my portfolio whilst investing and actively trading full-time. I believe in the three years leading up until then, I will be able to generate an adequate personal account; one in which I can make a living off of. If this plan does not work out, I will return to college and work towards a Masters degree in finance, all while interning and experiencing institutional investing. Once I recieve my MBA, I will either accept a job or if I see fit, I will return home to work on my personal portfolio. If I am to take the route of a career with a firm, I would likely only stay there for three years at most. My end goal is to become an independent investor, and I know it is possible with hard work and perserverance.


My Investing Goal

Investing is geniunely enjoyable to me. It is legitimately the first school-oriented activity that grabs my attention and makes me want to learn about it in my spare time. The only other subject that was relatively similar for me was coding, as I have been creating scratch projects since elementary school, but I saw the freedom skillful investing can bring someone, and I fell in love. I'm a natural introvert with a dismissive personality and therefore do not require everyday interaction and social situations. For me, the worst thing I could do is let myself become a pawn in the finance industry, catering to a higher-ups every demand. Although this all sounds very "cyberpunk" and contrarian, it is the path that I firmly believe will bring me life-long happiness. I have no desire to be a market wizard and make billions, but I do yearn for a free and sustainable life. A life where I can work while traveling the world, all the while enjoying what I do, sounds sublime.
